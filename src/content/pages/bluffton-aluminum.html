Aluminum Fencing is Versatile and Incredibly Durable

Many Designs At Your Disposal

From traditional fences to contemporary designs, you can have your pick of fencing styles in aluminum. Classic picket aluminum fences replicate the look of wood without the associated maintenance, while contemporary designs (such as geometric patterns and panels) offer options for modern homes in the Bluffton area.

Aluminum fences can be installed with tall privacy panels and reduced spacing between pickets to block visibility and access. Decorative designs can include intricate patterns, scrolls, and finials, and other touches.

Specific design examples include the spear-top for an elegant and secure boundary, the flat-top for a modern look, and the double-top with rings for something more ornamental. From imitation wood to imitation wrought iron, you have so many options to choose from.

You Have Your Choice of Color

We can install aluminum fences in many colors. There’s white, of course, if you want a traditional picket fence. We can also use glossy black finishes to replicate wrought iron or ornamental fencing, and you can even get dark green, bronze or other colors that are more ”earthy” in nature.

This is all because of the fascinating powder coat that is added to aluminum fencing. This advanced coating process applies a dry powder that is then cured under heat, creating a hard finish that is tougher than conventional paint. This method ensures the fence's color remains vibrant and resistant to fading, chipping, or peeling, even under harsh weather conditions, making aluminum fencing a lasting investment.

Is Aluminum Fencing Really That Durable?

Unlike iron, which can rust when exposed to moisture, or wood, which is susceptible to rot and insect damage, aluminum naturally resists corrosion. This makes it an ideal fencing material for a wide range of environments, including those with high humidity or salt air exposure. The inherent strength of aluminum ensures that it can withstand daily wear and tear, as well as occasional impacts, without bending or breaking. Moreover, the aforementioned powder coating process contributes to its durability. This protective finish wards off rust, corrosion, and color fading, effectively extending the lifespan of the fence.

Is It Harder To Install Aluminum Fences?

Unlike heavier materials that require specialized equipment for installation, aluminum is lightweight and easy to handle, which simplifies the process. Most aluminum fencing systems are designed with a modular setup, with pre-assembled panels and posts that fit together with relative ease. We don’t recommend trying to do it yourself, and our team of experts will align the posts perfectly, secure them in the ground with concrete, and make sure the whole thing is level and set up to last for many, many years.

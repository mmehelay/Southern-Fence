Vinyl is One Of The Best Values in Fencing

Vinyl Fences Can Last For a Long Time, Even in Bluffton

Typically, a vinyl fence can last anywhere from 20 to 30 years, depending on environmental conditions and the quality of the installation. With Southern Fence, you will receive only the very highest quality of installation, and we have seen these fences last for a very long time despite the storms that occasionally roll through the Lowcountry.

This durability is due in large part to the composition of the material itself. Polyvinyl chloride (PVC), the polymer used in the manufacture of vinyl fences, is known for its resistance to decay, rust, and insect damage. Unlike wood or metal fencing, which may require periodic treatments to maintain their integrity and appearance, vinyl stands the test of time with minimal upkeep. Forget rust, bugs, salty air, and rot – your vinyl fence will last for a long time.

You Have a Wide Choice of Colors and Finishes

Color is another significant advantage of vinyl fencing. The spectrum of colors available ranges from classic white to deep browns and even black or green. This variety allows property owners to choose fencing that complements the exterior of their homes or commercial buildings. The coloring process of vinyl fencing involves adding pigments during the manufacturing stage, which means the color goes through the entire depth of the material. This method ensures that scratches or nicks are less noticeable since the color remains consistent throughout the vinyl, not just on the surface.

You Won’t Have to Worry About Maintenance

Unlike wood, which may require staining or painting, or iron that might need rust treatment, vinyl fencing requires little more than occasional cleaning with soap and water to keep it looking new. This ease of care is particularly appealing to those who prefer not to spend time and money on routine maintenance tasks.

How Does Vinyl Stack Up, Pricing-Wise?

Vinyl is considered one of the best values in the fencing materials market. Initially, vinyl can be more expensive than wood but comparable to or less expensive than metal options like wrought iron or aluminum. However, when factoring in the absence of maintenance costs and the long lifespan of vinyl, it often reveals itself as a more cost-effective choice over time. The absence of a need for replacement or extensive upkeep can lead to significant savings, making vinyl an economically smart option in the long run. Also, it’s worth the investment, simply because it will save you time and hassle down the road.

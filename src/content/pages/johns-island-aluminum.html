Aluminum vs. Iron, Vinyl and Other Materials

Every fencing material has its pros and cons, but aluminum outshines many of its competitors. Traditional wrought iron may offer a similar aesthetic, but it’s heavy, expensive, and prone to rust—something aluminum never has to worry about. Wood fences look great initially, but they require constant maintenance to prevent warping, rotting, and insect damage. Vinyl, while low maintenance, can become brittle over time, especially in the fluctuating temperatures of the Lowcountry. Aluminum, on the other hand, stays strong and beautiful without the headaches of constant upkeep.

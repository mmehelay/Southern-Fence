Does Wooden Fencing Work in Adams Run?

Wood fencing is a great choice despite the coastal South Carolina climate. With the area's humidity and salty air, wooden fences need a little extra attention to keep them looking sharp. Treating the wood properly is very important; pressure-treated wood stands up well to moisture, while cedar and redwood naturally resist rot and insects. Regular staining or painting helps create a barrier against the elements and keeps the wood’s natural beauty intact. Routine maintenance, like cleaning and resealing every few years, adds to a wooden fence’s durability, allowing it to weather the seasons while looking its best. We are happy to stay in touch and maintain your fence for you, if you choose to install a wooden fence.

Aluminum Fences

Aluminum fencing is a great alternative for homeowners who want the look of wrought iron without the weight or cost. Aluminum fences often mimic the appearance of wrought iron while offering a high level of durability. This material resists corrosion and stands up well to humidity and salt in the air. Chain Link Fences

Chain link fencing offers practicality, security, and a low-profile look that works well for both residential and commercial properties. This fence type is budget-friendly, highly durable, and great for marking property lines, enclosing yards, or securing a garden area. Chain link fences are coated to resist rust and come in different colors, allowing them to blend with landscaping or stand out for added visibility. For those who want a bit more privacy, we can add slats in a range of colors to create a semi-private feel without losing the functional benefits.

Vinyl Fencing Installation
